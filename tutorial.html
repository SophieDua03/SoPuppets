tutorial

materials

10-12 gauge aluminium wire apoxie sculpt liquid laytex plastilina modeling clay or Sculpey clay the army/vallejo paint makeup sponges pilers/wire cutters tape aluminum foil nylon brushes

directions

step one

Draw out a 2D scale model of your desired puppet Draw the model life sized. Whatever it may be. Take your aluminium wire and twist pieces of wire until they are firm but bendable. Make sure that the wire is molded to the same height and length as the drawings arms and legs. *TIP* You can use thing wire and make hands too.

step two

Draw out a 2D scale model of your desired puppet Draw the model life sized. Whatever it may be. Take your aluminium wire and twist pieces of wire until they are firm but bendable. Make sure that the wire is molded to the same height and length as the drawings arms and legs. *TIP* You can use thing wire and make hands too.

step three

The limbs and body just need to be stable and immovable, but joints have to give the puppet the ability to move in a natural way. You will have to make joints as if you were trying to recreate a human being; ankles, wrists, shoulders, elbows, knees, neck etc.This can be done by using the apoxie scuplt which will harden in all the places that do not move.

step four

You can use either poley clay, plastilina or liquid laylex to mold and sculpt the skin and outer features of your puppet. You can even use a combination of all three. Laytex mixed with paint can create a very realistic texture of skin, Poley Clay is great for the body since it has a strong hold. Plastilina is great for the face, hair and any parts that my need to be remolded for movement since it doesnâ€™t harden.

step five

The best material for creating the head shape is aluminium foil since it is super easy to shape and manipulate for facial features like the noise, eye sockets, brow bone and mouth. After creating the shape you desire you can use either poley clay/plastilina clay or both to create the skin over the face.

step six

Unless you are using coloured clay, army or vellejo paint is the best high quality acrylic paint to paint the desired colour you need for your characters, skin and or clothing. Make sure that you are painting with artificial nylon brushes because real ones, will leave hair on your puppet.

step seven

Clothes can be sawn manually, which will give you most possibilities, but you could also buy dolls and toys and take their clothes to put on your puppet. Many different materials can work just make sure that they suit what will fit best with the personalty and style of your character.
